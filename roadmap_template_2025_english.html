Week 3-4

üóëÔ∏è Delete the Noise - Systematically

Conduct a digital detox process to rewire your brain:

  • Deactivate all non-essential notifications
  • Use noise-cancelling headphones for deep work
  • Keep digital devices out of reach during focus times
  • Design your workspace completely distraction-free
Implement protected time blocks: Reserve fixed times for strategic work and defend them uncompromisingly against meetings or interruptions.

üîÑ Delete Bad Habits - Substitution Method

Replace bad habits with better alternatives:

  • Instead of reaching for your phone ‚Üí Take 5 slow, deep breaths
  • Instead of procrastinating ‚Üí Apply the "Just Start" technique
  • Instead of perfectionism ‚Üí "Done is better than perfect"
  • Instead of multitasking ‚Üí Single-tasking with timer
"Use the 'Just Start' technique: Begin with a small part of the task to break through analysis paralysis."

3

Focus on Essentials

Ongoing

üèÜ The 3 Wins Method 3Fs Framework

Start each day with the question:

"What are my 3 Wins for today to achieve the greatest impact?"
  • Prioritize impact over activity
  • Intentionally allocate your energy to these 3 goals
  • Ensure your time aligns with what's most important
  • Track your daily wins for continuous motivation

üß† Establish Deep Work Blocks

Schedule blocks of uninterrupted time for deep work:

  • Identify your peak performance times during the day
  • Block 90-120 minutes for intense, focused work
  • Work on tasks that challenge you just right
  • Eliminate all interruptions during this time
  • Follow with short recovery breaks (15-20 min.)

Rule: No meetings, emails, or multitasking during Deep Work Blocks.

4

Develop Mastery

Quarterly Review

üìä Quarterly Breakdown

Break down your annual goals systematically:

  • Annual goals ‚Üí 4 quarters
  • Quarterly goals ‚Üí 3 months
  • Monthly goals ‚Üí 4 weeks
  • Weekly goals ‚Üí 5-7 days
Focus Rule: Set no more than 3 main goals per quarter. More leads to dilution and reduced effectiveness.

‚úÖ Define Actionable Steps

Break each goal into concrete, actionable steps:

  • Each step should be achievable in 1-4 hours
  • Set firm deadlines for each step
  • Focus on 1-3 tasks per day
  • Complete tasks fully before starting new ones

Principle: "Small wins lead to big victories" - Consistency beats intensity.

üìà Establish Success Metrics

Define measurable success criteria for each quarter:

  • Quantitative metrics (numbers, percentages, frequencies)
  • Qualitative metrics (customer feedback, skill level)
  • Leading indicators (process metrics)
  • Lagging indicators (outcome metrics)
"What gets measured gets improved. What doesn't get measured gets neglected."

Continuous Optimization

Daily & Weekly

üîÅ Daily/Weekly Habits

Build small, consistent actions that support your roadmap:

  • Morning routine: Define 3 Wins, set priorities
  • Midday check: Review progress, adjust
  • Evening routine: Reflect on day, prepare next day
  • Mind Dump: Get everything out, prioritize, execute 1-3 tasks

üéØ Process Orientation over Results Orientation

Focus on the quality of your process, not just outcomes:

  • Value and appreciate the steps to complete tasks
  • Celebrate small progress along the way
  • Learn from each step, not just final results
  • Avoid the temptation to skip essential steps
Mindset Shift: "I am someone who works on my goals every day" instead of "I must achieve this goal".

ü§ù Build Accountability

Create external commitment for increased dedication:

  • Find an accountability partner or mentor
  • Report weekly on your progress
  • Share your goals publicly (when appropriate)
  • Use tracking tools for transparency

üìù Weekly Review System

Every Sunday or Monday - structured weekly review:

Review Framework:
  • What went well? Document successes and wins
  • What went poorly? Identify obstacles and challenges
  • What did I learn? Capture insights and learnings
  • What will I change? Concrete adjustments for next week
  • Top 3 Priorities: Create clarity for upcoming week
"If I could only achieve one goal this year, which would have the greatest impact on my life?"

Core Principles for Success

  • Clarity before action: Understand your why before diving into the how
  • Less is more: 3 focused goals beat 10 diffuse wishes
  • Elimination as strategy: What you don't do is more important than what you do
  • Consistency beats intensity: Daily small steps accumulate to big results
  • Measure and adjust: What gets measured gets improved
  • Process over outcomes: Control the process, not the result
  • Accountability amplifies action: External commitment increases dedication
